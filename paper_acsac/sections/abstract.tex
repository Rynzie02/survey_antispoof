\begin{abstract}
Protective perturbations are a promising proactive defense against unauthorized voice cloning, 
yet attacks using diffusion purifiers can remove these perturbations before cloning, bypassing existing defenses. 
This gap arises because most protection methods optimize against the cloning model directly, treating purification only as an evaluation condition. 
We propose \method, a purification-aware voice protection framework that incorporates a surrogate diffusion purifier into the optimization loop. 
\method{} jointly maximizes pre-purification speaker-identity disruption, post-purification speaker-identity disruption, and trajectory deviation inside the reverse diffusion process, 
with the goal of keeping protected waveforms non-cloneable after purification-based recovery.
We operationalize the framework with purifier-in-the-loop optimization and ensemble-based speaker-identity objectives, 
and define a benchmark protocol covering direct protection, post-purification robustness, clean-purification controls, and perceptual quality. 
The completed evaluation will report post-purification SVA rates and speech-quality changes across [X] speakers, [Y] cloning backends, and [Z] target purifiers.
Our formulation reframes robust voice anti-cloning as an adaptive problem against the attacker's purification path,
rather than optimizing directly against the cloning backend alone.
\end{abstract}
